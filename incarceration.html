U.S. Incarceration Rate Comparison

This study compares the rate of incarcerated black people to the rate of total incarceration in federal, state and local prisons in the U.S.

The Rate of Incarceration in the U.S. has risen steadily since 1990, peaked in 2013, then slowly started to fall

  • Learn more

There is Little Correlation Between the Rate of the Incarcerated Black Population and the Total Incarcerated Population

This is illustrated by this graph, where the regression line is almost horizontal and there is high variance in the data points that fall far from the regression line.

  • Learn more

There is High Correlation Between the Rate of the Incarcerated White Population and the Total Incarcerated Population

In this graph, the regression line shows an upwards with much less variance in the data points.


Hypothesis: In the U.S. the rate of incarceration in the total population will not predict rate of incarceration in the Black population.

  • Learn more

Hypothesis is True

The rate of the incarcerated Black population is much higher than the rate of the total incarcerated population.

It may be important to note the sharp downward trend in the rate of the incarcerated Black population while the rate of the total incarcerated population is slowly rising.
